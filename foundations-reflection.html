What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

What I've taken on-board so far: the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming is to grow, develop and have a positive influence on yourself and the people around you.

Role of values (for an individual):

  • Values reflect what is important to us.
  • Values guide our beliefs, attitudes, behaviours, and expectations.
  • A values-based education puts an equal emphasis on personal and social development in relation to acquiring and processing knowledge and skills.
  • Understanding personal values helps people to develop personal and interpersonal capacity.
  • Realisation of values is crucial for the growth and existence of a person's sense of self (self-awareness).
  • Clarity about your values help creates a strong foundation for your life by providing a basis for decision-making and identifying actions which lead to happiness.

Role of empathy:

  • Empathy is to have a sense and awareness of what another is experiencing, ie. the capacity to place oneself in another's position.
  • Empathy is important to help us understand how others are feeling and so we can respond appropriately to the situation.
  • Empathy improves our capacity to communicate well with others and is essential for developing good relationships and resolving conflict.
  • Empathy is the foundation for many critical-thinking dispositions, also referred to as ‘intellectual virtues'.
  • Eg. “A critical thinker should be open to the ideas of others. This requires empathy, even to those who do not share your opinion. [Critical thinking] also requires you to be charitable when you evaluate other people's arguments. Again, this requires having enough empathy to not distort what other people are arguing, just as you would not want them to distort what you are saying.” - Jonathan Haber

References

Role of self-awareness

  • Self-awareness is the ability to focus on your self - one's emotions, thoughts, behaviors, values, preferences, goals, strengths, challenges, attitudes, mindsets, and so forth - and how these elements impact behavior and choices across contexts.
  • Being self-aware also includes thinking about how we treat others with empathy, and being aware of how others perceive us.
  • The Eurich group's research into the nature of self-awareness:
  • When we look inward, we can clarify our values, thoughts, feelings, behaviors, strengths, and weaknesses. We are able to recognize the effect that we have on others. Eurich's research finds that people with self-awareness are happier and have better relationships. They also experience a sense of personal and social control as well as higher job satisfaction.
  • When we look outward, we understand how people view us. People who are aware of how people see them are more likely to be empathetic to people with different perspectives. Leaders whose self-perception matches others' perceptions are more likely to empower, include, and recognize others.
  • Self-awareness is closely tied to self-management, which is the ability to navigate and shift in a healthy way one's thoughts, emotions, and behaviors in order to make decisions and reach goals that benefit oneself and others.
