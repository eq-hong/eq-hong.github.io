I posted super brief bulletpoints for strengths and limitations in sprint 2. My idea of these haven't changed.

Identifying new strengths: I'm pretty stubborn when I haven't understood something and if it feels important my brain won't stop spinning about it until I do understand it.

New limitations: I've found asking for help to be anxiety inducing, I have to feel like I've exhausted every avenue myself before asking. I found sometimes I don't have the energy to do just that and it results in paralysis/overthinking and it isn't time efficient.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

While you will face many technical challenges on Bootcamp, this is not what we would like you to consider in this plan. Non-technical challenges might include staying on top of the material, working in groups, speaking in front of the group, keeping focused for nine weeks, etc.

The first thing that does come to mind is in relation to keeping to a healthy sleeping routine. While working on my own I find my mind is sluggish in the morning (regardless of how much sleep I've gotten…) and much more efficient at night, which evolved into a later sleeping schedule over foundations. This may just be related to having freedom to manage myself. I had no issues focusing or working during mornings at my previous job, so when there is a more attendance-required structure in place I imagine I'll adjust to suit. I would like to start practicing a “close the day” ritual so I'm not lingering/stressing late into the night.
